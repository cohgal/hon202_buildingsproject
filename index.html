The Stephen Epler Hall was named after Portland State University's founder
Stephen E. Epler (1909-1997).
Also known as the Epler Residence Hall
the six-story building
serves the purpose of offering students
with exception to freshmen
residence year-round
The founder of Portland State University, Stephen Epler was born in Brooklyn, Iowa, in 1909.
He went on to attain a master’s degree in education from the University of Nebraska
and teach high school science in Chester, Nebraska.
In 1936, Epler moved to New York to begin doctoral studies.
After attaining a Ph.D. in educational sociology
and accepting a position as the dean of men
at Southern Oregon College in Ashland, Oregon in 1940,
he joined the U.S. Navy in 1943.
Upon returning to Oregon after World War II,
Epler was appointed veterans’ counselor in Oregon’s General Extension Division in Portland.
From there, he proposed and assembled the Vanport Extension Center, a public higher education center.
A decade later, he secured the necessary support to establish the VEC into a permanent four-year institution
now known as Portland State University.
