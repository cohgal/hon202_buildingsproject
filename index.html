Stephen Epler
About the man

The founder of Portland State University, Stephen Epler was born in Brooklyn, Iowa, in 1909. Epler, died July 12 in Sacramento at the age of 87.

He went on to attain a master’s degree in education from the University of Nebraska and teach high school science in Chester, Nebraska. In 1936, Epler moved to New York to begin doctoral studies. After attaining a Ph.D. in educational sociology and accepting a position as the dean of men at Southern Oregon College in Ashland, Oregon in 1940, he joined the U.S. Navy in 1943.

Epler
Later Life

Upon returning to Oregon after World War II, Epler was appointed veterans’ counselor in Oregon’s General Extension Division in Portland. From there, he proposed and assembled the Vanport Extension Center, a public higher education center. A decade later, he secured the necessary support to establish the VEC into a permanent four-year institution now known as Portland State University.
